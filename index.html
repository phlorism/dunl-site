Approach

Know the business. Build it right, build it fast, measure everything, iterate constantly. Apply that process to roadmaps and organizations — without sacrificing culture. Incorporate AI as far left as you're willing to go.

Product & Design Philosophy

Product and design are the same discipline. The best work happens when the person accountable for what gets built is also accountable for how it looks, feels, and performs. Separating strategy from craft creates handoff culture. Integrating them creates ownership.

Be right, and surround yourself with people who cover the rest. A product or design leader's job is to be right 98% of the time. Prioritize intelligently, learn fast, and build a team capable enough to catch what you miss. Kill or fix what doesn't work, even if the team loved building it.

Design systems are infrastructure, not artifacts. A component library isn't a deliverable — it's a living platform that either accelerates every team or slowly becomes debt. Govern it like a product: versioned, measured, and maintained by people who use it.

AI changes the ratio, not the job. AI makes production cheaper and faster. That means the human's job shifts from making things to judging things — evaluating AI output, maintaining quality standards, and making decisions that require context an agent can't have. The team gets smaller, not less skilled.

Culture is the product you can't A/B test. Process, tools, and rituals all matter — but the thing that actually determines whether a team ships great work is whether people feel safe disagreeing, trusted to make calls, and proud of what they're building.

Data tells you what happened. Feedback tells you why. Instrument everything, but don't hide behind dashboards. Talk to the people using what you built — guests, employees, partners. The metrics tell you where to look; the conversations tell you what to do about it.

Design Language

This site was designed and built in a single session using conversational AI. Every decision — color, type, spacing, motion — was intentional.

Color

Background
Foreground
Accent
Muted
Card
Border

Dark foundation with warm neutrals. Gold accent (#c9a96e) used sparingly for interactive elements, labels, and emphasis — never for backgrounds.

Typography

DM Serif Display

DM Sans — Light 300

Serif for headings and names — warmth and authority. Sans-serif at light weight for body — clean, modern, and readable. No bold in body text; hierarchy comes from size and color, not weight.

Motion

Scroll-triggered reveals with staggered delays. Timeline draws itself on scroll. Bouncing gold tracer on the hero divider. Cards lift on hover. All animations use ease-out curves and stay under 600ms. Nothing loops except the tracer — motion should feel responsive, not decorative.

Interaction

Everything that looks interactive is interactive. KPI cards expand on click. Outcome cards expand to reveal context. Shipped items open detail modals with impact bars. Photo opens a lightbox. The nav hides on scroll down, returns on scroll up. One easter egg for anyone paying attention.

Layout

Single column, 960px max-width. Generous but not wasteful spacing — 32px between sections. Content-first hierarchy: no decorative images, no illustrations, no filler. Section labels use extending lines to create horizontal rhythm across the full width.

Texture

Subtle SVG noise overlay at 4% opacity gives the dark background a tactile quality without being visible at a glance. Grain prevents the flat-black-screen feeling common to dark themes. Card surfaces sit one step lighter (#161614) to create depth without shadows.
